# Problem Title

# SIH 25006: Development of a Digital Farm Management Portal for Implementing Biosecurity Measures in Pig and Poultry Farms

# Proposed Solution
# Detailed Explanation of the Proposed Solution

The proposed solution is a Digital Farm Biosecurity Management Portal, designed to help pig and poultry farmers implement, monitor, and sustain effective biosecurity practices. The platform provides risk assessment tools, interactive training content, compliance tracking, and real-time alerts through a user-friendly and multilingual interface.

# The portal has four main components:

# 1. Dynamic Biosecurity Risk Assessment System

Tailored questionnaires for pig and poultry farms.

AI-based scoring to identify vulnerabilities (e.g., entry controls, waste disposal, equipment hygiene, visitor logs).

Region-specific risk indicators using local epidemiological data and disease outbreak reports.

# 2. Interactive Learning & Training Hub

Multimedia modules (videos, voiceovers, infographics) in multiple regional languages.

Step-by-step guides aligned with Government-approved biosecurity standards.

Mini quizzes and certification on completion to promote farmer engagement.

# 3. Digital Compliance & Record-Keeping

Daily/weekly checklists for farm sanitation, feed hygiene, vaccination, visitor entries, and waste management.

Auto-generated compliance reports for disease-free compartment recognition.

Cloud-based storage of historical farm data.

# 4. Real-Time Alerts & Monitoring Dashboard

Push notifications on disease outbreaks (e.g., Avian Influenza alerts from official sources).

AI-powered anomaly detection (sudden drop in feed intake, mortality pattern inputs).

Dashboard for farmers, veterinarians, and authorities to track farm risk status.

# 5. Stakeholder Networking & Support

Chat support for veterinarians and extension workers.

Community forums for farmers to share practices.

Policy data integration for authorities and researchers.

How It Addresses the Problem

Lack of awareness → Training modules, guidelines, multilingual videos.

Poor record keeping → Digital logs, automated compliance tracking.

Limited access to veterinary support → Teleconsultation and chat system.

Slow disease reporting → Real-time alerts and location-based outbreak notifications.

Resource constraints for small farmers → Free, mobile-first platform accessible via low-end devices.

Policy support → Aggregated analytics help authorities plan disease surveillance.

Innovation and Uniqueness of the Solution

AI-driven risk scoring tailored to local epidemiology (rare in rural farm tools).

Disease outbreak prediction using weather, seasonal patterns, and historical data.

Gamified training with certifications to boost farmer participation.

Offline-first mobile app for remote areas with poor connectivity.

End-to-end biosecurity lifecycle management (training → implementation → tracking → reporting → alerts).

Common interface for farmers, veterinarians, and policymakers to strengthen the livestock ecosystem.
